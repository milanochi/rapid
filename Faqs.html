Frequently Asked Questions(Faqs)

How do you apply and benefit?
Online Application Procedure The RAPID initiative loan application process would be online either directly or through accredited BDSPs. The process will ensure accessibility and efficiency for participants.
Process overview:
Preparation: Applicants gather necessary documentation including business plans and identification documents directly or through the accredited BDSPs.
1. Online Registration: Access the loan application portal, register by providing basic information, and create login credentials.
2. Application Form Submission: Complete the online application form with detailed business and project information, and upload supporting documents.
3. Document Verification: System verifies uploaded documents automatically; applicants may receive notifications for any discrepancies.
4. Review and Approval: BOIâ€™s State Office to conducts a thorough review of the application, evaluating project feasibility and creditworthiness and recommend at least to the selection committee for approval.
5. Decision and Notification: Applicants receive loan decisions via email or online portal, including terms and conditions if approved.
6. Acceptance and Disbursement: Review and accept loan offers; upon acceptance, sign the loan agreement. Funds are disbursed directly to suppliers upon supply of items.
7. Loan Management: Borrowers manage their loan accounts, make repayments, and access statements online. Project Officers provide ongoing support.
8. Monitoring and Reporting: Project Officers/BDSPs monitor loan performance, track repayments, and provide regular updates to ensure transparency and accountability.
However, for remote areas where internet access may be a challenge or where the applicant is not tech-savvy enough to navigate the online portal, completed application forms may be submitted physicaly at BOI State Offices.
What documents are required?
i. Completed Application Form
ii. Business registration documents
iii. Business Plan
iv. Acceptable Means of Identification
v. BVN and NIN
vi. Proof of Residential Address.
vii. Completed Guarantor Forms or deposit title documents
How are beneficiaries supervised?
Beneficiaries of the RAPID initiative are subject to supervision and oversight to ensure compliance with programme guidelines and objectives. The supervision process involves regular monitoring and evaluation activities conducted by relevant State Offices/BDSPs to assess the progress of beneficiaries, provide guidance and support as needed, and verify the effective utilization of programme resources.
